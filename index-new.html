About the Author

Les Kinney: Investigator. Researcher. Biographer.

Les has spent more than twenty-five years at the forefront of Amelia Earhart research. A former Treasury agent and Special Agent in Charge of multiple U.S. Customs field offices, his investigative career began with the Office of Naval Intelligence and later Treasury assignments in Washington, D.C., where he directed complex criminal investigations involving money laundering, organized crime, and weapons trafficking. He also served as liaison to the CIA, Pentagon, and State Department.

While stationed in Honolulu, Les developed intelligence-sharing networks across the Pacific. Multiple trips to remote islands exposed him to long-standing local accounts surrounding Earhart’s disappearance, transforming a professional curiosity into a decades-long investigation.

Les also served as assistant producer on the most-watched documentary ever made about Earhart’s disappearance, a project that still draws worldwide attention over her fate.

Upcoming Biography

A woman more complicated than the legend allowed.

Drawing on new archival discoveries, foreign travel, FOIA litigation, interviews, and on‑the‑ground fieldwork, Les Kinney re‑examines the woman behind the legend—peeling away the polished myths. Behind the smiling publicity shots lies a far more complicated story, obscured for decades by half‑told accounts, family coverups, abandonment, and biographers too eager to protect a legacy.

This is the Earhart story you’ve never heard: a tangled web of ambition, deception, and moral compromise stretching from the rocky beaches of Malibu to the streets of Manhatttan, with courtroom battles, hidden relationships, and a lawsuit that nearly destroyed the family’s name. With her flaws laid bare, Amelia Earhart emerges not as an untouchable icon, but unmistakably human.

And for the first time, this work turns to her disappearance with the same unflinching lens—revealing how the life she chose to live shaped her final flight into silence.

A Life Before the Silence (working title) opens the door to a second volume grounded in newly uncovered evidence. The first accurate telling of what transpired after Amelia and Fred Noonan vanished—a story some will not want to hear—a story that will "stagger your imagination."
